## Cosmology

### The Matter-Antimatter Asymmetry

The universe contains matter but almost no antimatter. The Standard Model contains CP violation — asymmetry between matter and antimatter — but not enough to explain the observed asymmetry. Something beyond the Standard Model generated the excess.

The octonionic T-asymmetry is structural — it's a theorem about the algebra, not a parameter. The early universe, before thermalization, would have been dominated by organized quantum states with geometric structure. Those states couple to spacetime through the octonionic channels.

### Dark Energy

The cosmological constant problem: quantum field theory predicts a vacuum energy 120 orders of magnitude larger than observed. Something cancels it almost perfectly. This is the worst fine-tuning problem in physics.

The path integral over perpendicular space paths gives a vacuum energy contribution from the phason modes. These modes are not present in standard matter — they are specific to systems with higher-dimensional geometric structure.

In the early universe, if matter had geometric structure (as it would before thermalization destroyed it), the phason vacuum energy contributed to the cosmological constant. As the universe cooled and geometric order was lost, the phason contribution disappeared.

This is not a solution to the cosmological constant problem. It is a new term in the calculation — a contribution from geometric order that standard QFT ignores because standard matter doesn't have it. Whether this term helps cancel the divergence or makes it worse depends on λ.

The quasicrystal measurement bounds the magnitude of the phason vacuum energy contribution in a laboratory system. Extrapolating to cosmological scales requires assumptions — but for the first time, those assumptions could be tested against a measured quantity rather than being purely theoretical.

### Inflation

Cosmological inflation — exponential expansion of the early universe — requires a scalar field (the inflaton) with a nearly flat potential. The inflaton is hypothetical. No particle corresponding to it has been observed.

It has produced major results (Fermat's Last Theorem, Sato-Tate conjecture) and remains largely conjectural in its general form.

### The Riemann Hypothesis Connection

The zeros of the Riemann zeta function are conjectured to correspond to eigenvalues of a self-adjoint operator — the "Hilbert-Pólya conjecture." The operator has never been constructed.

Random matrix theory — originally developed for nuclear physics — provides a statistical model that matches the distribution of Riemann zeros with extraordinary precision. The relevant random matrix ensemble is GUE — Gaussian Unitary Ensemble — which corresponds to systems with time-reversal symmetry broken.

The quasicrystal has broken time reversal. Its energy level statistics should follow GUE. But it also has octonionic structure — which goes beyond standard random matrix theory.

The energy level statistics of the E₈ root excitations, if they can be measured through careful spectroscopy, would provide a physical realization of the spectral statistics conjectured to underlie the Riemann zeros. This is not a proof — but it is an experimental probe of the arithmetic structure that the Riemann hypothesis concerns.

The E₈ lattice achieves the densest possible sphere packing in 8 dimensions — proved by Viazovska in 2016. The Leech lattice does the same in 24 dimensions. Both are intimately connected to the exceptional algebraic structures in the CHO hierarchy.
