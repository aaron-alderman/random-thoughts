# Survival Matrix Exploration
### From civilizational horizon back to the local conditions of staying alive

*A companion set to `ontology/1 - summary.md`, `ontology/6 - filter.md`, and `ontology/6a - coordination.md`*

---

> The transition fails if it cannot be lived locally. A project is not survival-oriented if it speaks in centuries while degrading food, trust, fertility, competence, repair capacity, or joy in the next five years.

---

## Preface: Why This Folder Exists

The ontology documents identify the large structural problem clearly enough:

- civilization generates surplus faster than home-world projects can absorb it
- surplus without shared project turns inward and becomes extraction, fragmentation, and closure
- species survival requires a transition toward a federated, space-capable civilization

What those documents do not yet do is walk the path back down the ladder. They do not yet specify what survival requires at the level where people actually live: households, neighborhoods, parishes, crews, schools, farms, clinics, workshops, towns, grids, and supply chains.

This folder exists to build that missing bridge.

It treats the problem as one system with many scales, not as separate issues to be solved one at a time. Meaning crisis, reproductive collapse, atomization, loss of skill, brittle supply chains, energy fragility, ecological overshoot, propaganda, institutional decay, and scientific drift are coupled failures. Any plan that isolates one and ignores the others will merely displace cost inside the same failing system.

---

## I. The Governing Rule

Every proposal in this folder is tested by one rule:

**If it improves long-horizon rhetoric while degrading near-term embodied survival, trust, reproduction, competence, or joy, it does not count as a survival solution.**

That rule has several consequences:

- the local and immediate are not optional preliminary concerns; they are the gating criterion for every higher-order proposal
- a plan that needs world government before anything meaningful can start is not a serious plan
- a plan that saves the species in theory but destroys the ordinary conditions of livable life in practice is self-refuting
- culture, fertility, celebration, craft, and child-rearing count as infrastructure, not decoration

---

## II. The Three-Layer Program

The transition is treated here as a three-layer program. The layers are sequential in dependence, but overlapping in time.

### Layer 1: Local survival and attachment

Keep people alive, attached, reproducing, skilled, and locally coordinated.

This includes:

- food, water, shelter, heat, cooling, and basic medical resilience
- child and elder care
- trust, repair, and conflict metabolism
- practical skill transmission
- local truth practices that resist propaganda and social hallucination
- shared beauty, ritual, and joy sufficient for ordinary people to want the project to continue

### Layer 2: Federated coordination without fiat

Build coordination and material surplus without flattening local remainder.

This includes:

- protocols rather than uniform authority
- shared stakes that the territory itself enforces
- institutions that allow local knowledge to propagate upward
- commons governance that makes defection costly without requiring civilizational dictatorship

### Layer 3: Surplus redirection toward the frontier

Redirect surplus into science, space capability, and a multi-generational shared project.

This includes:

- research and engineering that expand real capability rather than proxy prestige
- durable education and industrial capacity
- a spacefaring horizon that can absorb surplus at civilizational scale
- a story of the future that ordinary people can inhabit without despair or coercion

## IV. Evaluation Standard

An intervention belongs in this exploration only if it improves at least most of the following:

### 1. Embodied resilience

Does it increase the capacity to stay fed, housed, heated/cooled, defended, treated, mobile, and recoverable under stress?

### 2. Preservation of local remainder

Does it keep local knowledge, difference, and practical feedback alive, or does it flatten them into administratively convenient proxies?

### 3. Honesty and feedback

Does it improve contact with reality? Does it shorten feedback loops, expose failure quickly, and make error legible without humiliation theater?

### 4. Forgiveness and repair capacity

Does it increase the system's ability to metabolize error without permanent social fracture? Can people re-enter after mistakes? Can conflicts be repaired?

### 5. Shared-project orientation

Does it connect local effort to something larger than private extraction? Does it redirect surplus toward generative work instead of status games and internal predation?

### 6. Inhabitable joy

Can ordinary people live inside it gladly? Does it preserve courtship, family life, song, play, beauty, ritual, craft, and celebration?

If the answer is no on several of these, the proposal is likely a disguised proxy regime rather than a survival solution.

## V. Shared Terms

### Local-immediate survivability

The ability of a household or small community to remain physically intact and socially coherent through shocks in the next 0-5 years.

### Local cell

A small, durable group that can know one another personally, share burden, transmit skill, tell the truth, and attempt concrete projects together. Not an internet audience. Not an ideology. A load-bearing human unit.

### Federated coordination

Cooperation across larger scales through shared protocols, standards, auditing, mutual dependence, and real stakes, without requiring a single world-sovereign model.

### Shared project

A generative task large enough to absorb surplus, order institutions, and select for good-faith actors over time.

### Survival

Not mere pulse-preservation. Survival means continuity of the human capacity for attachment, child-rearing, judgment, skill, beauty, truthfulness, and frontier expansion.

## VI. How To Use This Folder

`1 - matrix.md` is the compressed map. It answers the same five questions for each scale-domain cell:

- What is failing now?
- What does collapse look like here?
- What minimum viable condition buys time?
- What institution or protocol could improve it?
- What 1-year experiment could start now?

The domain files then unpack those answers in a consistent schema:

- current failure pattern
- why this domain matters across all timescales
- minimum viable local condition
- scale-by-scale interventions
- practical bottleneck
- dependencies on other domains
- lead indicators
- failure modes
- open questions
- candidate pilot programs

`12 - sequencing and pilots.md` is the execution bridge. It moves from exploration to a first-wave prototype.

## VII. Non-Negotiable Constraints

This exploration rules out several fake solutions from the start:

- world government as the primary answer
- pure voluntarism with no real defection costs
- saving abstract humanity while letting actual families, neighborhoods, and institutions collapse
- technocratic proxy systems that destroy trust and local knowledge
- cultural austerity programs that treat art, celebration, fertility, and joy as luxuries

The transition will either be inhabitable by ordinary people or it will not happen.

---

## VIII. Initial Thesis

The path from the current world to a species oriented toward survival across all timescales starts here:

1. rebuild local units that can actually absorb pressure
2. connect them through federated protocols rather than fiat uniformity
3. use real shared problems as training grounds for coordination
4. redirect the surplus thus preserved into science, industry, and the frontier

The order matters. A civilization that cannot keep marriages intact, children welcomed, neighborhoods safe, clinics functioning, grids repairable, and truth practices alive will not survive long enough to become the steward of a spacefaring future.
