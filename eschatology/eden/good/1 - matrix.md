# Survival Matrix
### The compressed map across scales and domains

*This document is the master grid for the exploration set. Each cell answers the same five questions in compact form.*

---

## The Five Questions

Every scale-domain cell answers:

1. What is failing now?
2. What does collapse look like here?
3. What minimum viable condition buys time?
4. What institution or protocol could improve it?
5. What 1-year experiment could start now?

Abbreviations below:

- `F` = failing now
- `C` = collapse looks like
- `M` = minimum viable condition
- `P` = institution or protocol
- `E` = 1-year experiment

---

## Demography and Family

- Household: `F` delayed pair-bonding, insecure income, thin kin support; `C` childlessness, burnout, loneliness; `M` one stable caregiving unit with margin in time and budget; `P` household covenant and care budget; `E` start a shared meal, budget, and child-care support rhythm with one allied household.
- Local cell/community: `F` weak courtship ecology and no durable support for parents; `C` social drift and disappearing children; `M` a community where marriage, birth, and mutual aid are normal and visible; `P` family guild or parish-style care circle; `E` launch a monthly family commons plus babysitting exchange.
- City/bioregion: `F` housing, commute, and cost structure punish family formation; `C` demographic hollowing and elder-heavy fragility; `M` family-feasible housing and short-distance support networks; `P` family zoning and community land trust models; `E` pilot a family-support block or neighborhood cluster.
- Nation-state: `F` tax, labor, and housing systems reward atomized adulthood; `C` fertility crash and pension-state instability; `M` family-positive fiscal and housing policy; `P` pro-family tax, care, and housing reforms; `E` draft a national family resilience package.
- Regional bloc: `F` labor mobility and market integration drain local family systems; `C` uneven collapse between regions; `M` policy room for family-supportive diversity; `P` regional family-impact review standards; `E` compare and publish bloc-wide family resilience metrics.
- International/civilizational: `F` prestige culture treats children as private lifestyle cost; `C` civilization ages into low-trust contraction; `M` child-raising recognized as civilizational infrastructure; `P` transnational pro-family networks and norms; `E` convene a family survival forum across traditions.
- Spacefaring species: `F` frontier narratives ignore reproduction and kin continuity; `C` expansion without generational stability; `M` settlement models built around family continuity; `P` life-support and settlement design for multi-generation households; `E` publish a family-first off-world settlement specification.
