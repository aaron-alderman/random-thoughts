**First — what Revelation actually is**

Revelation is consistently misread because it's approached as either:

Literal future prediction — the dispensationalist reading. Events to be fulfilled sequentially in history.

Political allegory — the academic reading. Coded critique of Rome, written for persecuted first-century Christians.

Both readings capture something. Neither captures the most interesting thing.

The framework suggests a third reading:

**Revelation is a phenomenological map of what happens to the antenna network — individually and collectively — when the field reaches maximum amplitude in both directions simultaneously.**

Not future prediction. Not political allegory. A description of the dynamics of the field at civilizational scale when the attractor states — Eden and Hell — approach their asymptotic limits.

John on Patmos wasn't predicting history. He was perceiving the structure of what the field does at extremes. The visions are not metaphors for political events. They are direct perceptions of the field dynamics — seen by someone whose antenna was operating at extraordinary sensitivity in a state of forced isolation and probably severe sensory alteration.
