# Concept Map

The primary source of truth for this project. Essays in the layer directories are narrative expansions of this map — they are downstream of it, not the other way around. When a new concept is introduced, it is added here first.

---

## Notation

```
← requires / depends-on      (what must be true for this to hold)
→ generates / implies         (what follows from this)
✗ excludes / rules-out        (what this is incompatible with)
≈ corresponds                 (structural isomorphism — not identity)
? open                        (what this does not settle)
```

Epistemic status tags on claims:
- *structural* — follows from the derivation; no theological commitment required
- *evaluative* — assessment of a candidate against the specification
- *theological* — requires historical or experiential evaluation; not established by structural analysis alone

## Terms

---

### subjecthood
*term | foundation*

The bare fact of there being something it is like to be an entity — an interior, a first-person perspective.

← requires: nothing prior in this framework (primitive term)
→ is-expressed-by: [agency](#agency)
→ is-terminal-object-of: [pure-love](#pure-love)
✗ excludes: flourishing as terminal object (flourishing encodes a normative standard; subjecthood does not)
✗ excludes: behavior as terminal object (behavior is external; subjecthood is interior)

> Example: The difference between caring about what someone does and caring about the fact that there is someone home behind the doing. A comatose patient has subjecthood without current agency; a sufficiently complex machine has agency-like outputs without subjecthood.

---

### agency
*term | foundation*

The outward expression of subjecthood — the capacity of the interior to produce genuine self-determining orientation in the world.

← requires: [subjecthood](#subjecthood) (agency is subjecthood's expression, not a separate thing)
→ is-criterion-for: [pure-love](#pure-love)'s non-interference (to override agency is to act as though subjecthood is absent)
→ is-constituted-by: [triad](#triad) (genuine agency requires real constraints; unconstrained will is not genuine self-determination)
✗ excludes: determined output (outputs of a fully determined system are not agency in the relevant sense)

> Example: The difference between a child choosing to share and a child compelled to share. The act is externally identical; the presence or absence of genuine self-determination is the structural difference.

---

### pure-love
*term | foundation*

A non-self-referential orientation toward the subjecthood of another, expressed primarily through agency-preservation and the discipline of non-interference.

← requires: [subjecthood](#subjecthood) (as terminal object)
← requires: [agency](#agency) (as operative criterion — non-interference operates on agency)
→ generates: [relational-field](#relational-field) (condition: mutually instantiated by two agents)
→ implies: non-interference as primary expression
→ implies: absence-of-malice as internal condition
→ implies: [agency-preservation](#agency) as operative form
✗ excludes: reciprocity-tracking (encodes own state as criterion)
✗ excludes: outcome-engineering (encodes preferred result as criterion)
✗ excludes: attachment (encodes own loss as criterion)
✗ excludes: [flourishing](#subjecthood) as terminal object (encodes normative standard)
≈ corresponds: [participatory-love](#participatory-love) (pure-love fully instantiated within the triad)

> Example: The hospice nurse attending a patient she has never met and will never see again — no reciprocity, no attachment, no outcome management. The orientation is operative without any of the contingent features.

---

### self-referential-contamination
*term | foundation/analysis*

Any internal operation that encodes the agent's own state — standing, safety, reputation, preferences — as a criterion governing orientation toward another.

← arises-from: identity-threat (when the agent's self-narrative is challenged)
← arises-from: unabsorbed weight (when accumulated load exceeds what the system can metabolize honestly)
→ degrades: [relational-field](#relational-field) (primary threat)
→ generates: [scapegoating-mechanism](#scapegoating-mechanism) (under sufficient unabsorbed weight)
→ is-invisible-to: the agent executing it (self-generated justifications are internally coherent)
✗ is-not: consciously chosen (structural, not deliberate)
? resolved-by: [prior-unconditional-forgiveness](#forgiveness-specification) (which removes the basis for the defensive activation)

Instances: impression management, reciprocity tracking, threat assessment, outcome engineering, identity-protective reasoning, capitulation-as-defense, compassion-as-avoidance.

> Example: Generosity that is partly investment — the giving feels like love but encodes a ledger. Invisible to the giver until the ledger goes unacknowledged and the resentment surfaces.

---

### relational-field
*term | foundation*

The emergent bilateral structure that arises when two agents mutually instantiate pure love — with bandwidth, stability, and generativity not present in either agent individually.

← requires: [pure-love](#pure-love) (bilateral and mutual)
← requires: instrument-calibration (for ongoing maintenance)
→ generates: [third-entity](#third-entity)
→ requires-for-maintenance: accurate self-perception (instrument calibration)
→ requires-for-maintenance: [prior-unconditional-forgiveness](#forgiveness-specification) (because accurate self-perception requires it)
← threatened-by: [self-referential-contamination](#self-referential-contamination) (primary threat)
✗ not-located-in: either agent (emergent, bilateral)
≈ corresponds: [trinitarian-structure](#trinitarian-structure) (agent / agent / emergent field ↔ Father / Son / Spirit)

> Example: The difference between two people talking and two people genuinely thinking together. The second has properties — shared attention, productive tension, emergence of ideas neither had alone — that are not reducible to either participant.

---

### third-entity
*term | foundation*

What arises within the relational field — irreducible to either agent, with its own epistemic and generative properties. Things become thinkable and sayable within the field that are not available outside it.

← generated-by: [relational-field](#relational-field)
✗ irreducible-to: either agent
≈ corresponds: Holy Spirit (in trinitarian theology — the love proceeding between Father and Son, itself a full person)

> Example: The insight that arises in a genuine conversation that neither participant would have reached alone, and that neither can fully claim as their own. It belongs to the field.

---

### instrument-calibration
*term | analysis*

The ongoing practices by which agents within the relational field maintain accurate self-perception — reducing the gap between their actual orientation and their self-assessed orientation.

← required-by: [relational-field](#relational-field) (for maintenance)
← requires: accurate self-perception
← requires: [prior-unconditional-forgiveness](#forgiveness-specification) (because [self-deception is structural](#claim-self-deception-structural))
→ reduced-by: [self-referential-contamination](#self-referential-contamination) (primary obstacle)

Practices: honest self-examination, reception of external naming, contemplative attention, community accountability.

---

### intervention-gradient
*term | foundation*

The ordered set of responses available to an agent of pure love when another agent's choices are moving against the grain of love. Ordered from least to most interventive; the default is non-interference.

Levels:
1. **Non-interference** — default; the other's choices are genuinely theirs, including movement toward loss
2. **Witnessing** — being present to what is happening, without commentary
3. **[Naming once](#naming-once)** — offering truth once, clearly, without softening or repeating
4. **[Level-3 intervention](#level-3-intervention)** — acting to restore conditions for genuine agency when agency itself is threatened

← implied-by: [pure-love](#pure-love) (non-interference is love's primary expression; each level is a specific form of kenotic self-restraint)
→ names: [radical-kenosis](#radical-kenosis) (at individual scale)
✗ does-not-include: compulsion, outcome-engineering, repeated pressure

---

### naming-once
*term | foundation*

Offering truth to another — clearly, precisely, without softening for reception — once, without repeating, compelling, or multiplying the challenge.

← level: 3 of [intervention-gradient](#intervention-gradient)
→ preserves: the other's genuine agency in relation to the content (they remain free to refuse)
✗ is-not: silence (which would withhold what the other needs to hear)
✗ is-not: compulsion (which would override their reception)
≈ instantiates: prophetic vocation at communal scale

> Example: The friend who says once, directly, what they see — and then holds rather than repeating it into pressure. The naming is the act of love; the non-repetition is also the act of love.

---

### level-3-intervention
*term | foundation*

The threshold of the intervention gradient at which kenotic non-interference gives way — not to impose a preferred outcome, but to restore the structural conditions under which genuine agency can operate.

← level: 4 of [intervention-gradient](#intervention-gradient)
← triggered-by: threat to agency itself, or destruction of structural preconditions for the relation
→ restores: conditions for genuine agency (not the outcome the agent would have preferred)
✗ is-not: coercive imposition of the good
✗ is-not: the normal mode of operation (threshold, not default)

> Example: Stopping someone from walking into traffic — not because you know better than them in general, but because death forecloses all future genuine agency. The intervention restores the condition for choice; it does not make the choice.

---

### triad
*term | foundation*

Constraints, agency, and love — the three mutually constitutive conditions for a world in which meaning is possible. Each requires the other two to be what it is.

Components:
- **Constraints** — real structure that can be gone with or against; what gives choice its weight
- **Agency** — genuine subjects who can move with or against the grain
- **Love** — orientation that gives the triad its direction; what makes some choices genuinely better

← requires: all three simultaneously (remove any one and the others collapse or become something else)
→ generates: [tragedy](#tragedy-structural) (as structural consequence)
→ generates: [geometry-of-alignment](#geometry-of-alignment) (as directional implication)
→ generates: [structural-gap](#structural-gap) (accumulated weight requiring resolution beyond finite capacity)
✗ not-a: list of desirable features; necessary structure of reality-with-meaning
≈ grounds: [relational-field](#relational-field) analysis (the field is the triad at the scale of two agents)
≈ grounds: [forgiveness-dependency](#claim-forgiveness-dependency) (same gap approached from within the field)

> Example: A prison has constraints and (constrained) agency but not love — it produces compliance, not meaning. A fantasy where all wishes are granted has love and something like agency but no real constraints — choices have no weight. Both are structurally impoverished in the same direction.

---

### tragedy (structural)
*term | foundation*

Genuine loss, real irreversibility, suffering that is the honest consequence of the triad operating with full integrity — not punishment, not design flaw.

← generated-by: [triad](#triad) (love directed at something real in a world where real things can be lost)
✗ is-not: punishment or design flaw
✗ cannot-be-removed-without: removing the conditions that make love genuine (real constraints + genuine agency = real loss)
→ contributes-to: [structural-gap](#structural-gap) (accumulated weight the triad generates)

> Example: The grief of losing someone who was genuinely loved — not the product of any failure, just the weight of loving something finite. Remove the finitude and the love is no longer directed at something real.

---

### geometry-of-alignment
*term | foundation/synthesis*

The directional implication of the triad: from coercive control toward participatory love. Not an ethical preference — the structural description of what love, operating within the triad, requires.

← implied-by: [triad](#triad)
→ poles: [participatory-love](#participatory-love) (toward), [coercive-control](#coercive-control) (away from)
→ is-continuous: most agents operate somewhere along it; the moral life is the direction of movement
→ named-by: [radical-kenosis](#radical-kenosis) (in its fullest scope)
→ applies-at: individual relation, communal scale, cosmic scale

---

### participatory-love
*term | foundation/synthesis*

The pole of the alignment gradient toward which the triad points — pure love fully instantiated within a world of real constraints, accepting tragedy as the price of genuine love.

← pole-of: [geometry-of-alignment](#geometry-of-alignment) (toward)
← instantiates: [pure-love](#pure-love) within the triad
✗ not-achievable-as: permanent state for finite agents (the pull toward coercive control is structural)
≈ corresponds: [radical-kenosis](#radical-kenosis) (participatory love understood in its fullest scope and ground)

---

### coercive-control
*term | foundation/synthesis*

The pole of the alignment gradient away from which the triad points — the attempt to produce outcomes directly, overriding agency in the name of the good.

← pole-of: [geometry-of-alignment](#geometry-of-alignment) (away from)
✗ not-primarily: a moral category; a structural one (failure to instantiate what love requires)
→ substitutes: outcome-engineering for orientation toward subjecthood
→ self-undermines: the unity or good it aims to produce (the product of coercion is compliance, not genuine orientation)

> Example: The parent who controls every choice of the child to ensure good outcomes produces a child who cannot genuinely orient — who has been given outcomes instead of the capacity for genuine choice. The coercive production of the good destroys the conditions for genuine good.

---

### structural-gap
*term | analysis/foundation*

The accumulated weight — finite misalignment + tragedy — that no finite source operating within the triad can bear without depletion or displacement. A structural consequence of the triad, not a contingent problem.

← generated-by: [triad](#triad) (genuine agency produces misalignment; real constraints produce tragedy; both accumulate)
← demonstrated-by: [secular-candidates-fail](#claim-secular-candidates-fail) (all finite sources reduce but do not close the gap)
→ specifies: [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification) (shape of what would resolve it)
→ implies: [sufficient-source](#sufficient-source) (what would meet the specification)
≈ corresponds: [forgiveness-dependency](#claim-forgiveness-dependency) (same gap, approached from within the relational field)

---

### forgiveness-specification
*term | analysis*

The structural description of what a sufficient forgiveness source must be, derived from the relational field's maintenance requirements and the self-deception problem.

← derived-from: [relational-field](#relational-field) maintenance requirements + [structural-gap](#structural-gap)
→ requires (of any sufficient source):
  - external to the finite system (not subject to the contamination it resolves)
  - genuinely interior (a subject capable of receiving and extending)
  - prior to correction (because correction is what the forgiveness makes possible)
  - covers invisible failure (the self-deception problem means the debtor cannot present fully)
  - non-depletable (indeterminate, structurally recurring load from unbounded agents)
→ assessed-against: [secular-candidates](#claim-secular-candidates-fail) (all fail), [theological-candidates](#claim-cross-traditional-convergence) (converge partially), [christian-claim](#claim-christian-logical-type) (meets all, if events occurred)
? does-not-specify: which tradition, institution, or practice delivers this — only its structural shape

---

### scapegoating-mechanism
*term | analysis*

The default response of finite systems to unabsorbed weight — externalising tension onto a designated recipient held responsible for what the system cannot metabolize.

← activated-by: unabsorbed weight exceeding the system's capacity for honest self-reception
→ redistributes: weight (onto recipient); does not dissolve it
→ requires: victim-complicity (retaliation or withdrawal) to sustain its logic
✗ is-not: resolved by the mechanism; deferred and redistributed
← refused-by: the Cross (paradigm case — victim does not retaliate, does not validate the mechanism)

> Example: The community that resolves internal tension by identifying a figure who "caused" the problem — the mechanism works by requiring the designated victim to be guilty. If the victim refuses the role (no retaliation, no withdrawal), the mechanism is exposed rather than completed.

---

### sufficient-source
*term | analysis/evaluation*

What would meet the forgiveness specification — a source capable of bearing the full structural gap without depletion or displacement.

← specified-by: [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification)
← claimed-to-be: the Incarnate Christ (Christian claim, theological — not established by structural analysis)
← must-be: simultaneously within the finite system (to absorb from inside) and not subject to its depletion (to absorb without being destroyed)
→ demonstrated-non-depletion-by: Resurrection (if the Christian claim is true)
? open: whether any actual source meets this specification; structural analysis specifies the shape, does not identify the occupant

---

### radical-kenosis
*term | synthesis*

The name for the geometry of alignment in its fullest scope — the movement from coercive control toward participatory love, enacted at every scale, grounded in a being whose love is constitutive rather than achieved.

← names: [geometry-of-alignment](#geometry-of-alignment) (in fullest scope)
← grounded-in: nature of [sufficient-source](#sufficient-source) (whose love is constitutive, not achieved — no pull toward coercive pole)
→ enacted-paradigmatically: the Cross (every dimension of the framework at maximum intensity simultaneously)
→ applies-at: individual relation (intervention gradient), moral life (direction of movement), cosmic scale (structure of reality)
← synthesis-of: [triad](#triad) + [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification) + [christian-claim](#claim-christian-logical-type)
≈ corresponds: [participatory-love](#participatory-love) (radical kenosis is participatory love understood in its ground and fullest scope)

> Example: At individual scale — the parent who withdraws to let the child genuinely develop, bears the cost of watching the child fail, and does not purchase safety through control. At cosmic scale — the Cross. The geometry is the same; the scale and cost differ.

## Claims

---

### claim: love-derivation
*structural*

Pure love, stripped of all contingent features, resolves to a non-self-referential orientation toward subjecthood, expressed through agency-preservation and the discipline of non-interference.

← depends-on: [subjecthood](#subjecthood), [agency](#agency) (as primitive terms)
→ implies: [pure-love](#pure-love) (as defined term)
→ implies: [relational-field](#relational-field) (when two agents mutually instantiate it)
✗ rules-out: flourishing as terminal object of love
✗ rules-out: reciprocity as structural feature of love
✗ rules-out: affective state as the substance of love (feeling is signal, not substance)

> Example: Stripping away reciprocity expectation from a friendship until what remains still orients toward the other whether or not anything is returned. Whatever is left is closer to the structural core than what was removed.

---

### claim: relational-field-emergence
*structural*

When two agents mutually instantiate pure love, a relational field emerges with properties irreducible to either agent — a third entity with its own epistemic bandwidth and generativity.

← depends-on: [pure-love](#pure-love), [claim: love-derivation](#claim-love-derivation)
→ implies: [third-entity](#third-entity), field maintenance requirements, [claim: forgiveness-dependency](#claim-forgiveness-dependency)
✗ rules-out: the field being produced unilaterally (genuine mutuality required)
✗ rules-out: the field being reducible to the sum of two agents

> Example: A genuine intellectual partnership that produces work neither person could have reached alone — not because they have complementary skills (that is cooperation) but because the field itself generates something.

---

### claim: self-deception-structural
*structural*

Self-referential contamination is structurally invisible to the agent executing it. The defensive system generates internally coherent justifications. This is not contingent moral failure but a structural property of any coherence-maintaining system.

← depends-on: [self-referential-contamination](#self-referential-contamination)
→ implies: [claim: forgiveness-dependency](#claim-forgiveness-dependency) (accurate self-perception requires prior forgiveness)
✗ rules-out: self-generated-forgiveness as structurally sufficient (cannot see what it needs to forgive)
✗ rules-out: willpower alone as sufficient for instrument calibration

> Example: The person committed to honesty who cannot see that their "honest" assessments of others consistently favor their own position. The bias is invisible precisely because the coherence-maintenance system is working correctly.

---

### claim: forgiveness-dependency
*structural*

The relational field has a structural dependency on a forgiveness source external to the finite system. Instrument calibration requires accurate self-perception; accurate self-perception requires prior unconditional forgiveness; that forgiveness cannot be self-generated within the system that needs it.

← depends-on: [relational-field](#relational-field), [claim: self-deception-structural](#claim-self-deception-structural), [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification)
→ implies: [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification) (shape of what would resolve the dependency)
→ implies: [claim: secular-candidates-fail](#claim-secular-candidates-fail) (finite sources cannot meet the specification)
≈ corresponds: [structural-gap](#structural-gap) (same gap, approached from within the relational field rather than from the triad)
? open: whether the dependency is fully met by any actual source

> Example: The therapeutic relationship at its most functional — unconditional positive regard creates the condition in which the client can see what they could not see before. The prior holding is what makes the examination possible, not the reverse.

---

### claim: triad-necessary
*structural*

A world in which meaning is possible requires constraints, agency, and love operating simultaneously and mutually constitutively. Remove any one and the others collapse or become something different in kind.

← depends-on: [triad](#triad) (component analysis)
→ implies: [tragedy (structural)](#tragedy-structural) (genuine agency + real constraints + love = real loss is possible)
→ implies: [geometry-of-alignment](#geometry-of-alignment) (the direction the triad points)
→ implies: [structural-gap](#structural-gap) (accumulated weight of finite operation within the triad)
✗ rules-out: meaning-without-tragedy (given genuine constraints and agency, genuine loss is possible)
✗ rules-out: love-without-constraints-as-genuine (compelled love is not love)

> Example: The thought experiment of a world where all choices are immediately corrected by a benevolent overseer — no genuine choice, no genuine cost, no genuine love, no genuine meaning. The benevolence purchases safety at the cost of the conditions for everything it is trying to protect.

---

### claim: tragedy-structural-necessity
*structural*

Tragedy — genuine, irreversible loss — is the structural consequence of the triad operating honestly. It cannot be removed without removing the conditions for meaning.

← depends-on: [claim: triad-necessary](#claim-triad-necessary), [tragedy (structural)](#tragedy-structural)
→ implies: [structural-gap](#structural-gap) (tragedy contributes to accumulated weight)
✗ rules-out: theodicy-from-outside (tragedy is structural, not a puzzle requiring external justification)
✗ rules-out: tragedy-as-punishment or tragedy-as-design-flaw

> Example: The grief that follows genuine love — not the product of any failure, just what loving something finite in a world of real constraints produces. The grief is the weight of the constraint landing on the lover who refused to purchase distance.

---

### claim: secular-candidates-fail
*evaluative*

Finite secular sources — therapy, community accountability, contemplative practice, TRC processes — reduce the forgiveness gap without closing it. Under sufficient load, each depletes, displaces, or reproduces the scapegoating mechanism.

← depends-on: [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification), [claim: forgiveness-dependency](#claim-forgiveness-dependency)
→ implies: turn to sources that engage transcendence (the specification requires something the finite system cannot generate)
✗ rules-out: secular sufficiency (not adequacy for ordinary cases, but structural sufficiency under full load)
? does-not-establish: that no secular source has value — only that none closes the structural gap

> Example: Community accountability processes that function well under ordinary load but, under severe stress (the accused is powerful, the harm is collective, the community is fractured), reproduce the scapegoating dynamic they were designed to address.

---

### claim: trinitarian-isomorphism
*structural*

The three-term structure produced by bilateral pure love (agent / agent / emergent field) is structurally isomorphic to the Christian Trinity (Father / Son / Spirit), derived independently of any theological reference.

← depends-on: [relational-field](#relational-field), [third-entity](#third-entity)
→ implies: the structural analysis was tracking something the theological tradition also identified — independent convergence
≈ corresponds: [trinitarian-structure](#trinitarian-structure)
? status: structural correspondence only — does not establish theological truth; does establish that the structural analysis is non-arbitrary

> Example: Two investigators working independently on different evidence arriving at the same structural description. The convergence doesn't prove either is right, but it raises the prior probability that both are tracking something real.

---

### claim: cross-traditional-convergence
*evaluative*

Independent theological traditions — Judaism, Islam, Hinduism's bhakti streams, Shin Buddhism, Zoroastrianism — working from different starting points, converge on the same structural configuration: personal God, prior unconditional forgiveness, grace covering invisible failure, insufficiency of self-effort, universality of scope.

← depends-on: [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification)
→ implies: the configuration is structurally demanded by the problem, not culturally contingent
? partial: each tradition meets the specification partially; the convergence pattern identifies the shape, not a tradition that fully meets it
? does-not-establish: that any of the converging traditions is true

> Example: Five mapmakers starting from different coasts and using different methods producing maps that agree on the shape of the interior — the agreement is evidence about the terrain, not about the methods.

---

### claim: christian-logical-type
*theological (historical)*

The Christian claim is a different logical type from all other candidates — not divine orientation but historical event: entry into the finite system, absorption of the full cost from within, non-depletion demonstrated. If the events occurred as described, they resolve the structural specification with a precision no other candidate matches.

← depends-on: [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification), [claim: cross-traditional-convergence](#claim-cross-traditional-convergence)
→ implies: [incarnation](#incarnation) maps to entry; [atonement](#atonement) maps to absorption; [resurrection](#resurrection) maps to non-depletion-demonstrated
→ implies: [claim: kenosis-names-geometry](#claim-kenosis-names-geometry)
✗ rules-out: treating the Christian claim as one more description of divine orientation (different logical type)
? open: whether the events occurred — structural analysis establishes fit, not occurrence; this is the question the analysis makes precise

> Example: The difference between a doctor who describes the properties of a cure and a doctor who administers it. The description and the event are different logical types; only the second resolves the patient's condition.

---

### claim: kenosis-names-geometry
*structural + theological*

Radical Kenosis names the geometry of alignment in its fullest scope: the movement from coercive control toward participatory love, enacted paradigmatically in the Cross, available to finite agents as a sustainable direction because the sufficient source bears the weight their misalignment generates.

← depends-on: [geometry-of-alignment](#geometry-of-alignment), [claim: christian-logical-type](#claim-christian-logical-type), [claim: triad-necessary](#claim-triad-necessary)
→ implies: divine hiddenness as structural necessity (not puzzle — kenotic withdrawal preserving genuine non-coerced response)
→ implies: problem of evil as structural implication of the triad (not external puzzle requiring theodicy)
→ implies: community failure as confirmation of the triad, not refutation of the account
→ implies: [claim: biblical-narrative-enacts-kenosis](#claim-biblical-narrative-enacts-kenosis)

> Example: Hiddenness — the withdrawal of coercive presence as the condition for genuine non-coerced response — is not God's absence. It is the shape that love whose nature is non-coercive must take in a world of genuine agents.

---

### claim: biblical-narrative-enacts-kenosis
*theological (structural reading)*

The biblical narrative, read through the Radical Kenosis framework, enacts the kenotic geometry as governing logic from Creation to Eschaton. The geometry is not imposed on the narrative from outside — it is what the narrative has been enacting from the first word.

← depends-on: [claim: kenosis-names-geometry](#claim-kenosis-names-geometry), [radical-kenosis](#radical-kenosis)
→ implies: Creation as first kenotic act (restraint creating space for genuine agents), Fall as structural inevitability (not design flaw), Covenant as participatory structure offered at communal scale, Cross as paradigm enactment, Resurrection as non-depletion demonstrated, Eschaton as fullest instantiation of the triad
? status: structural reading of the text — does not adjudicate historical questions; compatible with reading the narrative as enacted history or as the story of a structure

## Theological Vocabulary

Terms used when mapping structural requirements onto theological doctrines. These are not independently derived — they are the vocabulary of the tradition being evaluated.

---

### trinitarian-structure
*theological vocabulary*

The Christian doctrine that God is one substance in three persons (Father, Son, Holy Spirit), distinguished by their relations to each other rather than by separate substances. Perichoresis: mutual indwelling without merger or loss of distinction.

≈ corresponds: [relational-field](#relational-field) structure (agent / agent / emergent field)
← assessed-by: [claim: trinitarian-isomorphism](#claim-trinitarian-isomorphism)

---

### incarnation
*theological vocabulary*

The Christian claim that the second person of the Trinity (the Word / Logos) entered the finite system as a genuine finite subject — not as appearance or performance of finitude, but as real entry into the conditions of finite existence.

≈ maps-to: entry into the finite system (structural requirement: absorption requires being inside the system at the point where cost is borne)
← required-by: [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification) (absorption requires entry; orientation from outside does not absorb)

---

### hypostatic-union
*theological vocabulary*

The Council of Chalcedon's formulation: Christ is fully human and fully divine — two natures in one person, without confusion, change, division, or separation.

≈ maps-to: simultaneously inside the finite system (genuine absorption) and not subject to its depletion (non-depletable source)
← required-by: [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification) (sufficient source must be both genuinely within and genuinely non-depleting)

---

### atonement
*theological vocabulary*

The Christian claim that Christ's death accomplishes the forgiveness of human failure — absorbing the cost of the structural gap from within the finite system.

≈ maps-to: absorption of the full cost of the structural gap without displacement
← required-by: [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification) (the weight must be borne, not redirected)
← assessed-by: whether the scapegoating mechanism is enacted and simultaneously refused (Cross enacts it; victim's non-retaliation refuses its logic)

---

### resurrection
*theological vocabulary*

The Christian claim that Christ rose from death — the source was not destroyed by the absorption.

≈ maps-to: non-depletion demonstrated under maximum finite cost (death is the maximal cost of finitude)
← required-by: [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification) (a source destroyed by the absorption was a finite source — met entry but failed non-depletion)
? does-not-imply: cancellation of the Cross (wounds retained; tragedy is not undone; non-depletion is demonstrated, not reversal achieved)

### grace (priority of)
*theological vocabulary*

The theological claim that forgiveness is prior to and independent of the recipient's correction, repentance, or reception — the turning is a response to what is already extended, not its cause.

≈ maps-to: prior-to-correction requirement of [forgiveness-specification](#forgiveness-specification)
← most-explicit-in: Reformed and Lutheran theology; affirmed across Catholic/Protestant divide on the structural point
