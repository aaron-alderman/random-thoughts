# Part IX - Key Properties Reference

## Big Picture

The broader landscape makes repeated use of a relatively small set of material properties. This document keeps those properties in one place so later planning can stay fast without losing conceptual grounding. It is intentionally reference-like rather than essay-like.

Even as a reference, the big picture still matters: these properties are not isolated metrics. They are practical markers of how strongly phonons couple into charge flow, structural response, heat transport, and more exotic emergent behavior.

## This Document Covers

This document provides a compact reference for the key electrical, thermoelectric, mechanical, piezoelectric, thermal, caloric, and emerging properties highlighted elsewhere in the landscape, along with the main design insight or open problem attached to each one.

## Electrical and Thermoelectric

| Property | What it indicates | Main design insight or open problem |
|---|---|---|
| Seebeck coefficient | Voltage generated by a temperature gradient | Strong values often appear in semiconductors and near phase transitions; conductivity tradeoff remains central |
| Nernst effect | Transverse voltage from longitudinal heat flow in magnetic settings | Anomalous Nernst geometries may be better for harvesting than standard Seebeck layouts |
| Phonon drag | Momentum transfer from phonons to electrons | Can strongly enhance Seebeck response at low temperature; room-temperature retention is still open |

## Mechanical and Piezoelectric

| Property | What it indicates | Main design insight or open problem |
|---|---|---|
| Piezoelectricity | Coupling between stress and polarization | Requires broken inversion symmetry; biology and lead-free replacements remain important opportunities |
| Flexoelectricity | Polarization from strain gradients | Exists even in centrosymmetric materials and grows at small thickness, making nanoscale systems especially important |
| Triboelectricity | Charge transfer under contact or separation | Mechanism remains debated; surface state, humidity, and geometry dominate behavior |

## Thermal

| Property | What it indicates | Main design insight or open problem |
|---|---|---|
| Thermal rectification | Directional asymmetry in heat flow | Often small in practice; still central to heat-diode and thermal-circuit ambitions |
| Negative thermal expansion | Contraction upon heating | Shows that thermal response can be geometrically counterintuitive and highly material-specific |
| Thermal Hall effect | Transverse heat transport | Phonon-mediated versions in insulators remain conceptually puzzling and experimentally important |

## Caloric

| Property | What it indicates | Main design insight or open problem |
|---|---|---|
| Magnetocaloric effect | Temperature change under changing magnetic field | Strongest near magnetic transitions; low-field room-temperature materials remain the main challenge |
| Elastocaloric effect | Temperature change under stress or strain | NiTi is promising for cooling, but fatigue remains the core bottleneck |
| Twistocaloric effect | Temperature change under torsion | Demonstrated recently and still barely mapped across material classes |

## Exotic and Emerging

| Property or domain | What it indicates | Main design insight or open problem |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanocaloric behavior | General temperature change under pressure, strain, or twist | A broader umbrella for solid-state cooling pathways |
| Electrochemical actuation | Volume change driven by ion insertion | Usually treated as a battery-side problem, but may enable useful actuator platforms |
| Osmotic energy conversion | Energy available from salinity gradients | Large global potential; membrane selectivity and pore control are decisive |

## How To Use This Reference

Use this document when you need a quick answer to three recurring questions:

- What property actually matters for this effect?
- Why is that property difficult to optimize?
- What unresolved design variable makes the area interesting?

For deeper mechanism, interpretation, or measurement strategy, move outward to the linked concept, methods, and project documents.
