**Title**
Spin(2,3)–octonion dynamics and Standard Model structure: a unified framework and phenomenological implications

---

**Abstract**

We present a unified framework in which spinor dynamics based on Spin(2,3) and internal structure derived from the octonion algebra Octonions jointly organise key features of Standard Model fermions. Building on a projected dynamical principle, in which observables are defined on a time-oriented sector while evolution occurs in an extended space, we show that inter-sector mixing yields an effective open-system evolution with a diffusive component. The same geometric framework reproduces SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) representation structure through the reduction of G₂ and spinor decomposition. We identify a relation between effective diffusion strength and fermion mass parameters, and discuss phenomenological implications, including constraints on transport behaviour and potential deviations from standard quantum evolution at small scales. The framework provides a unified geometric setting linking representation structure and effective dynamics.

---

**1. Introduction**

Understanding both the representation structure and dynamical behaviour of Standard Model fermions within a unified framework remains a central challenge. Previous work has shown that exceptional algebraic structures and spin groups can encode aspects of internal symmetries, while open-system approaches provide effective descriptions of dissipative phenomena.

In this work we combine these perspectives. Building on a projected spinor dynamics derived from Spin(2,3) and an internal structure based on the octonions Octonions, we propose a framework in which both fermion representation content and effective dynamical behaviour arise from a common geometric origin.

---

**2. Geometric framework**

The construction is based on two ingredients:

1. A spinor (\Psi \in \mathcal{H}_1 \oplus \mathcal{H}_2) associated with Spin(2,3), admitting a decomposition into two sectors.

2. An internal space derived from the octonions Octonions, whose automorphism group G₂ reduces to SU(3) upon selection of a preferred direction.

The combined structure yields representations of SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1), organising fermion multiplets.

---

**3. Projected dynamics and sector mixing**

We adopt the dynamical principle:

* evolution occurs in (\mathcal{H}_1 \oplus \mathcal{H}_2)
* observables are defined by projection onto (\mathcal{H}_1)

The evolution equation is taken as

$$
i\partial_t \Psi = (H_0 + m K)\Psi,
$$

where (K) induces transitions between sectors.

Eliminating the complementary sector yields an effective evolution for the reduced density matrix (\rho_1) of the form

$$
\dot{\rho}_1 = -i[H_1, \rho_1] + \mathcal{D}(\rho_1),
$$

where (\mathcal{D}) is a dissipative term.

---

**4. Diffusive limit and mass relation**

In a semiclassical limit, the effective evolution reduces to

$$
\partial_t \rho + \nabla \cdot (v \rho) = D \nabla^2 \rho.
$$

The diffusion coefficient depends on the inter-sector coupling:

$$
D \sim \frac{m^2}{\gamma},
$$

where (\gamma) characterises relaxation in the complementary sector.

This relation suggests a link between effective transport properties and fermion mass parameters.

---

**5. Standard Model representation structure**

The representation content arises from the combined geometric structure:

* SU(2) doublets from spinor decomposition of Spin(2,3)
* SU(3) colour from reduction of G₂
* U(1) charge from a combined generator acting on both sectors

This yields representations consistent with quark and lepton multiplets. Consistency conditions associated with anomaly cancellation select corresponding singlet states.

---

**6. Phenomenological implications**

The framework suggests several qualitative implications:

1. **Mass–diffusion relation**
   Effective diffusion strength is linked to fermion mass parameters. Heavier fermions correspond to stronger coupling between sectors.

2. **Modified transport at small scales**
   At sufficiently short timescales, deviations from purely unitary evolution may appear due to unresolved sector transitions.

3. **Suppression of coherence**
   Sector mixing induces effective decoherence, with a rate controlled by the same parameter governing mass.

These effects are expected to be small in regimes where standard quantum dynamics is well tested.

---

**7. Limitations**

The present framework does not yet include:

* a fully relativistic field-theoretic formulation
* explicit gauge field dynamics
* quantitative predictions for experimental observables
* a derivation of coupling constants

The phenomenological statements are therefore qualitative.

---

**8. Discussion**

The results suggest that both representation structure and effective dynamical behaviour may originate from a common geometric setting. In particular, the identification of mass with inter-sector coupling provides a link between algebraic structure and transport properties.

The framework differs from conventional open-system approaches in that the complementary sector is intrinsic to the spinor representation rather than an external environment.

---

**9. Conclusion**

We have presented a unified framework combining projected spinor dynamics and octonionic internal structure. The construction organises Standard Model representations and yields an effective dynamical description in which mass is associated with sector mixing and diffusion arises from hidden-sector dynamics.

These results point toward a geometric perspective in which both symmetry structure and effective dynamics emerge from a common underlying framework.
