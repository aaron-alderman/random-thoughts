# Phenomenology Kernel

## Purpose

This document is the phenomenology source text for the `j3_oc` branch.

Its job is to say:

- what phenomenological consequences, if any, survive the ambient-to-observable reduction
- what remains only suggestive
- what would be needed before the branch supports a real phenomenology paper

This file must stay especially modest. The branch is ambient-first, so phenomenology should only appear after reduction discipline.

---

## Scope

This file covers:

- branch-level phenomenological ambitions
- what would count as phenomenological progress here
- why reduced `spin2_3` phenomenology cannot simply be copied over

This file does not cover:

- reduced `spin2_3` scaling laws as if they were already derived here
- sharp quantitative bounds
- numerical predictions not yet earned by the branch

---

## Core phenomenological question

The central question is:

> what observable difference would the complexified ambient branch eventually make?

At present, the honest answer is:

- not much has been exported safely yet

That is because the phenomenology of `j3_oc` is downstream of:

1. admissibility
2. reduction
3. observable-slice definition

Without those, ambient richness is not phenomenology.

---

## What cannot simply be imported

The branch should not automatically inherit the following as its own phenomenology:

- `T1/T2` transport results
- Lindblad reduction claims
- diffusion scaling such as `D ~ m^2 / gamma`

Those belong to the reduced branch unless and until `j3_oc` supplies a clean derivation path to them.

The safe use of such results here is:

- downstream target
- comparison case
- possible effective image after successful reduction

---

## Candidate phenomenological directions

If the branch matures, the most plausible phenomenological directions would be:

1. consequences of how the observable slice is selected from the ambient complexified arena
2. consequences of whether structural `SU(3)` really survives as physical color
3. consequences of whether `u` is derived or inserted
4. consequences of whether mixing acquires a geometric meaning not present in the reduced branch alone

These are phenomenological directions only in a preparatory sense. They are not yet predictions.

---

## Safe phenomenological statements at present

The branch can safely say:

1. a successful ambient branch should eventually change what counts as an observable reduced explanation
2. if `j3_oc` improves the reduction story, that could later yield phenomenological differences from simpler branches

The branch cannot yet safely say:

- that it has sharp observables
- that it has numerical predictions
- that it already improves on reduced-branch phenomenology

## Major unresolved issues

The phenomenology domain still owes:

1. a clear mapping from branch structure to observables
2. a demonstration that the branch earns something beyond ambient reinterpretation
3. a statement of where it would differ from `J3(O)` or reduced `spin2_3`
4. a statement of what could actually be checked

---

## Working bottom line

The phenomenological spine of `j3_oc` is currently very thin.

At its safest level, it says:

1. phenomenology must wait for reduction
2. reduced-branch results are comparison targets, not ambient achievements
3. any future phenomenology must come from what the complexified branch uniquely earns

That is the right level of caution for now.
