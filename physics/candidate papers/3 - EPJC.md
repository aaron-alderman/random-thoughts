**Title**
Spin(2,3)–octonion geometry and Standard Model representations

---

**Abstract**

We present a geometric framework in which key features of Standard Model fermion representations arise from the combined structure of Spin(2,3) and the octonion algebra Octonions. The spinor representation of Spin(2,3) decomposes into SU(2) doublets with a natural U(1) grading, while the automorphism group of the octonions, G₂, reduces to SU(3) upon selection of a preferred imaginary direction. Combining these structures yields representations corresponding to quark and lepton doublets with the correct SU(3)×SU(2) transformation properties and fractional charge assignments. Consistency conditions associated with anomaly cancellation select a corresponding set of singlet representations. The framework also accommodates three generations through the structure of the exceptional Jordan algebra J₃(𝕆) and its triality symmetry. We emphasise that the construction organises Standard Model representation content geometrically, without addressing gauge dynamics.

---

**1. Introduction**

The fermion content of the Standard Model exhibits a highly structured pattern of representations under the gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1). While this structure is well established experimentally, its geometric origin remains an open question.

Exceptional algebraic structures, particularly those involving the octonions Octonions and their automorphism group G₂, have long been known to encode aspects of internal symmetries. Separately, spin groups such as Spin(2,3) provide a natural setting for fermionic degrees of freedom.

In this work we combine these two ingredients to show that Standard Model representation structures can be organised within a unified geometric framework. The emphasis is on representation content rather than dynamics.

---

**2. Spin(2,3) spinor structure**

The 4-component spinor of Spin(2,3) decomposes under its maximal compact subgroup as

$$
\mathbf{4} = (\mathbf{2}, q_-) \oplus (\mathbf{2}, q_+),
$$

where (\mathbf{2}) denotes the fundamental representation of SU(2) and (q_\pm) are eigenvalues of a U(1) generator.

Each sector therefore carries the structure of an SU(2) doublet with a definite U(1) charge.

---

**3. Octonionic internal space and SU(3)**

The octonion algebra admits a decomposition

$$
\mathbb{O} = \mathbb{R} \oplus \mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{O}),
$$

where the imaginary part transforms under the automorphism group G₂.

Selecting a preferred imaginary unit reduces G₂ to SU(3). Under this subgroup, the imaginary octonions decompose as

$$
\mathrm{Im}(\mathbb{O}) = \mathbf{3} \oplus \bar{\mathbf{3}} \oplus \mathbf{1}.
$$

The triplet and singlet components provide a natural distinction between colour-carrying and colour-neutral degrees of freedom.

---

**4. Combined representation structure**

We consider the tensor product of the Spin(2,3) spinor sector with the octonionic internal space. Restricting to one sector of the spinor decomposition, we obtain representations of the form

$$
(\mathbf{2}) \otimes (\mathbf{3} \oplus \mathbf{1}).
$$

This yields:

* ((\mathbf{3}, \mathbf{2})): colour triplet SU(2) doublets
* ((\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{2})): colour singlet SU(2) doublets

These correspond structurally to quark and lepton doublets.

---

**5. Charge assignments**

A U(1) generator can be constructed as a linear combination of:

* the Spin(2,3) U(1) generator
* a generator acting on the octonionic decomposition

Fixing coefficients to match observed relative charges yields assignments consistent with fractional charges for triplet states and integer charges for singlet states.

We emphasise that this procedure determines relative charge structure rather than absolute normalisation.

---

**6. Singlet sector and consistency conditions**

The doublet representations alone are not sufficient for consistency in a chiral gauge theory. Imposing cancellation of gauge anomalies constrains the allowed singlet representations.

The minimal set consistent with these constraints consists of colour triplet singlets and colour singlet singlets with appropriate charge assignments, matching the pattern observed in the Standard Model.

We interpret this as a consistency condition on the representation content rather than an independent geometric derivation.

---

**7. Three generations from J₃(𝕆)**

The exceptional Jordan algebra J₃(𝕆) consists of 3×3 Hermitian matrices with octonionic entries. Its off-diagonal components transform under triality, a symmetry associated with Spin(8).

The three off-diagonal entries are permuted cyclically under this symmetry, suggesting an organisation of three equivalent copies of the representation structure described above.

This provides a natural geometric interpretation of the existence of three generations.

---

**8. Discussion**

The construction presented here organises several features of Standard Model fermion representations within a unified geometric framework:

* SU(2) doublet structure from spinor decomposition
* SU(3) colour from octonion automorphisms
* fractional charge assignments from combined U(1) structure
* replication into three generations via triality

The framework does not address:

* gauge field dynamics
* symmetry breaking
* mass hierarchies

Its role is to provide a geometric organisation of representation content.

---

**9. Conclusion**

We have shown that combining the spinor structure of Spin(2,3) with the internal structure of the octonions Octonions yields a natural organisation of Standard Model fermion representations. The resulting structure reproduces key features of quark and lepton multiplets and accommodates three generations through the exceptional Jordan algebra J₃(𝕆).

These results suggest that exceptional geometry may provide a useful organising principle for particle representation structure.
