# The Standard Model Gauge Structure from Spin(2,3) and the Octonions

## Abstract

We derive the Standard Model gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) and the matter representations of one generation — and exactly three generations — from two geometric inputs: the Lie group Spin(2,3) and the octonion algebra 𝕆. The 4-component spinor of Spin(2,3) decomposes under its maximal compact subgroup U(1)×SU(2), giving the weak isospin and hypercharge structure of left-handed fermions. The octonion automorphism group G₂, reduced to SU(3) by a choice of preferred imaginary unit e₇, provides the colour degree of freedom. The combined T1-sector representations reproduce exactly Q_L = (3,2)_{+1/6} and L = (1,2)_{-1/2} with hypercharge identified as Y = J^{01} + 2Q₇. Anomaly cancellation uniquely determines the right-handed fermion content. Three generations arise from the triality automorphism of the Albert algebra J₃(𝕆), with a fourth generation algebraically excluded. The entire construction requires a single canonical input — a choice of U(1)_Y inside Spin(2,3)×G₂ — which simultaneously determines the time arrow, the colour structure, and all hypercharge values. The group Spin(2,3)×G₂ embeds in E₆ via the triality decomposition of Spin(8) as the stabiliser of a primitive idempotent of J₃(𝕆). The Standard Model gauge group and matter content are consequences of this geometry, not inputs to it.

## 1. Introduction

The Standard Model of particle physics is one of the most precisely tested theories in science, yet its gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) and matter representations appear to be chosen rather than derived. Three generations of fermions, the specific hypercharge assignments, anomaly cancellation — these are consistent but their geometric origin is obscure.

This paper addresses that obscurity directly. We show that the Standard Model gauge structure and matter content for one generation, replicated exactly three times, follow from a specific geometric framework: the spinor representation of Spin(2,3) combined with the internal structure of the octonions.

The framework uses two well-established mathematical objects. The first is the Lie group Spin(2,3), the covering group of SO(2,3) — the isometry group of a 5-dimensional spacetime with two time dimensions and three space dimensions. The physical interpretation of the second time dimension is developed in a companion paper [Part I]; here we use only the group-theoretic structure. The second object is the division algebra of octonions 𝕆, whose automorphism group G₂ contains SU(3) as the stabiliser of a preferred imaginary unit.

The paper proceeds in seven steps. Section 2 constructs the Clifford algebra Cl(2,3) and the explicit gamma matrices. Section 3 decomposes the 4-component spinor under the maximal compact subgroup K = U(1)×SU(2). Section 4 attaches the octonion internal space and identifies the colour and hypercharge structure. Section 5 verifies anomaly cancellation and derives the right-handed fermion content. Section 6 establishes three generations from the triality automorphism of the Albert algebra J₃(𝕆). Section 7 proves the embedding Spin(2,3)×G₂ ⊂ E₆ and states the central theorem.

Throughout, we distinguish carefully between established mathematics we are using (Tier 1), geometric claims of the framework (Tier 2), and derived consequences (Tier 3). The Tier 1 results are: the classification of normed division algebras, the identification Aut(𝕆) = G₂, the stabiliser SU(3) ⊂ G₂, the isomorphism so(2,3) ≅ sp(4,ℝ), and the Jordan-von Neumann-Wigner classification of formally real Jordan algebras.

## 2. The Clifford Algebra Cl(2,3)

### 2.1 Conventions

We work in ℝ^{2,3} with metric

$$\eta^{\mu\nu} = \text{diag}(-1,-1,+1,+1,+1)$$

with indices μ = 0,1,2,3,4, where μ = 0,1 are timelike and μ = 2,3,4 are spacelike. The Clifford algebra Cl(2,3) is generated by matrices γ^μ satisfying

$$\{\gamma^\mu, \gamma^\nu\} = 2\eta^{\mu\nu} \cdot \mathbf{1}_4$$

The minimal faithful representation acts on a 4-component complex spinor.

### 2.2 Explicit Gamma Matrices

Let σ¹, σ², σ³ denote the standard Pauli matrices. A valid representation of Cl(2,3) is:

$$\gamma^0 = i\sigma^2 \otimes \mathbf{1}_2, \qquad \gamma^1 = i\sigma^1 \otimes \mathbf{1}_2$$

$$\gamma^2 = \sigma^3 \otimes \sigma^1, \qquad \gamma^3 = \sigma^3 \otimes \sigma^2, \qquad \gamma^4 = \sigma^3 \otimes \sigma^3$$

These satisfy {γ^μ, γ^ν} = 2η^{μν} with the correct signature. The timelike generators γ⁰, γ¹ square to −1 and the spacelike generators γ², γ³, γ⁴ square to +1.

### 2.3 The Spin Generators

The Lie algebra spin(2,3) is spanned by

$$J^{\mu\nu} = \frac{i}{4}[\gamma^\mu, \gamma^\nu]$$

giving 10 generators matching dim(SO(2,3)) = 10. The generator of rotations in the (t₁,t₂) plane is:

$$J^{01} = \frac{i}{4}[\gamma^0, \gamma^1] = -\frac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix} \mathbf{1} & 0 \\ 0 & -\mathbf{1} \end{pmatrix}$$

with eigenvalues:

$$J^{01} = \text{diag}\left(-\tfrac{1}{2}, -\tfrac{1}{2}, +\tfrac{1}{2}, +\tfrac{1}{2}\right)$$

The 4-component spinor splits into two eigenspaces: components (ψ₁,ψ₂) with eigenvalue −1/2 and components (ψ₃,ψ₄) with eigenvalue +1/2. These are the **T1-sector** and **T2-sector** respectively.

## 3. Decomposition Under the Maximal Compact Subgroup

### 3.1 The Subgroup K = U(1) × SU(2)

The maximal compact subgroup of Spin(2,3) is

$$K = U(1) \times SU(2)$$

The U(1) is generated by J^{01}. The SU(2) is generated by rotations among the spacelike directions:

$$J^{23} = -\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{1}\otimes\sigma^3, \qquad J^{24} = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{1}\otimes\sigma^2, \qquad J^{34} = -\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{1}\otimes\sigma^1$$

These satisfy the SU(2) algebra [J^{23}, J^{24}] = iJ^{34} and cyclic permutations. They act **identically** on both 2-component blocks of the spinor.

### 3.2 The Decomposition

The 4-component spinor decomposes under K as:

$$\mathbf{4} = (\mathbf{2}, -\tfrac{1}{2}) \oplus (\mathbf{2}, +\tfrac{1}{2})$$

where the notation is (SU(2) representation, U(1) charge).

| Components | SU(2) | U(1) charge |
|---|---|---|
| ψ₁, ψ₂ | doublet **2** | −1/2 |
| ψ₃, ψ₄ | doublet **2** | +1/2 |

The SU(2) acts as −(1/2)σ^a on each block. The U(1) charge is the J^{01} eigenvalue. There is no SU(3) at this stage — colour requires the octonion structure.

## 4. The Octonion Internal Space and the Colour Structure

### 4.1 The Octonions

The octonions 𝕆 form the unique 8-dimensional normed division algebra (Hurwitz 1898). As a vector space:

$$\mathbb{O} = \text{span}\{e_0, e_1, \ldots, e_7\}$$

with e_0 the real unit and e_1,...,e_7 imaginary units satisfying e_ie_j = −δ_{ij} + ε_{ijk}e_k, where ε_{ijk} are the Fano plane structure constants (nonzero for triples 124, 235, 346, 457, 561, 672, 713).

### 4.2 The Automorphism Group and SU(3)

The automorphism group of 𝕆 is the exceptional Lie group G₂. Selecting a preferred imaginary unit e₇ breaks G₂ to the stabiliser SU(3) ⊂ G₂. Under SU(3), the remaining imaginary units decompose as:

$$\{e_1,\ldots,e_6\} = \mathbf{3} \oplus \bar{\mathbf{3}}$$

with triplet basis q^a = (e_a + ie_{a+3})/√2 and antitriplet q̄^a = (e_a − ie_{a+3})/√2 for a = 1,2,3. The unit e₇ is the SU(3) **singlet** by construction.

### 4.3 The Spacetime-Octonion Correspondence

The 5-dimensional spacetime ℝ^{2,3} and the octonion algebra share a natural correspondence:

| Spacetime ℝ^{2,3} | Octonion 𝕆 | Role |
|---|---|---|
| t₁ | e₀ | Time arrow / real unit |
| t₂ | e₇ | Virtual time / fixed imaginary |
| x₁, x₂, x₃ | e₁, e₂, e₃ | Space / colour triplet |
| — | e₄, e₅, e₆ | Internal / colour antitriplet |

Under this identification, t₁ maps to the real unit e₀ — the algebraically distinguished direction fixed by all of G₂ — and t₂ maps to e₇ — the preferred imaginary unit whose stabiliser is SU(3). The time arrow and the colour structure are two aspects of the same octonionic decomposition.

### 4.4 The Combined Representation Space

We take the combined space for one generation of matter to be:

$$\mathcal{H}_{\text{gen}} = \Psi_{T1} \otimes (\mathbf{3} \oplus \mathbf{1})$$

where the **3** (quark sector) and **1** (lepton sector) come from the SU(3) decomposition of the imaginary octonions, and Ψ_{T1} is the T1-sector spinor. The T2-sector is identified with the chirality and mass coupling mechanism developed in Part I [citation].

The antitriplet **3̄** is not an independent sector. Under the Hermitian constraint of J₃(𝕆), the **3̄** in position (i,j) is the conjugate of the **3** in position (j,i). Physical degrees of freedom are counted by the upper triangular entries only.

### 4.5 The Triality Grading Q₇

Define the triality operator Q₇ by its eigenvalues on each SU(3) sector:

| Sector | Q₇ eigenvalue |
|---|---|
| **3** (quarks) | +1/3 |
| **3̄** (antiquarks) | −1/3 |
| **1** (leptons) | 0 |

This operator is real, diagonal, commutes with SU(3), and has normalization fixed by requiring the triplet to carry the minimal nonzero charge. The factor of 1/3 — and hence the fractional electric charges of quarks — enters directly from the octonion structure.

### 4.6 The Hypercharge Identification

The hypercharge generator is:

$$Y = J^{01} + 2Q_7$$

The coefficients are fixed uniquely by two conditions. From the lepton doublet (SU(3)=1, SU(2)=2, J^{01}=−1/2, Q₇=0):

$$\alpha(-\tfrac{1}{2}) = -\tfrac{1}{2} \implies \alpha = 1$$

From the quark doublet (SU(3)=3, SU(2)=2, J^{01}=−1/2, Q₇=+1/3):

$$-\tfrac{1}{2} + \beta(+\tfrac{1}{3}) = +\tfrac{1}{6} \implies \beta = 2$$

No other values of α and β are consistent with both conditions simultaneously.

### 4.7 The T1-Sector Matter Content

The T1-sector (J^{01} = −1/2) tensored with the octonion internal space gives:

| Sector | SU(3) | SU(2) | Y | SM identification |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | **3** | **2** | +1/6 | Q_L (quark doublet) |
| C | **1** | **2** | −1/2 | L (lepton doublet) |

These are exactly the left-handed SM representations for one generation.

### 4.8 The u₊ Projection

The Peirce idempotent u₊ = (e₀ + e₇)/2 gives a state with Y = +2, which has no SM counterpart. This state is projected out by the Jordan algebra structure: its e₀ component maps to a **diagonal entry** of J₃(𝕆), which carries no spinor index, no colour, and no SU(2) quantum numbers. Diagonal entries of J₃(𝕆) are real scalars — they are not matter states. The e₇ component of u₊ is already accounted for in the T2-sector lepton singlet.

**Lemma:** Physical matter states are those whose octonionic component lies in the imaginary non-fixed directions (triplet q^a) or the fixed imaginary direction (singlet e₇), corresponding to quarks and leptons respectively. States with e₀ components are projected out by the Jordan algebra structure.

## 5. Anomaly Cancellation and the Right-Handed Fermion Content

### 5.1 The Anomaly Conditions

For a chiral gauge theory with gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1)_Y, anomaly cancellation requires:

| Condition | Requirement |
|---|---|
| [SU(3)]²U(1)_Y | Σ Y over SU(2) components of triplets = 0 |
| [SU(2)]²U(1)_Y | Σ Y over colour components of doublets = 0 |
| [U(1)_Y]³ | Σ Y³ = 0 |
| Gravitational | Σ Y = 0 |

### 5.2 The T1-Sector Alone

The T1-sector contains Q_L = (3,2)_{+1/6} contributing 6 Weyl fermions and L = (1,2)_{-1/2} contributing 2 Weyl fermions.

| Condition | T1 result |
|---|---|
| [SU(3)]²U(1)_Y | 2(+1/6) = +1/3 ≠ 0 ✗ |
| [SU(2)]²U(1)_Y | 3(+1/6) + (−1/2) = 0 ✓ |
| [U(1)_Y]³ | −2/9 ≠ 0 ✗ |
| Gravitational | 6(+1/6) + 2(−1/2) = 0 ✓ |

Two conditions fail. This is not a failure of the framework — it is a constraint. The T1-sector alone is anomalous, which means additional representations are **required** by consistency.

### 5.3 The Required Right-Handed Content

The unique set of representations that restores anomaly cancellation is:

$$u_R = (\mathbf{3},\mathbf{1})_{+2/3}, \qquad d_R = (\mathbf{3},\mathbf{1})_{-1/3}, \qquad e_R = (\mathbf{1},\mathbf{1})_{-1}$$

Verification with the full generation (right-handed fermions entering with opposite sign as left-handed antifermions):

| Condition | Full generation |
|---|---|
| [SU(3)]²U(1)_Y | +1/3 − 2/3 + 1/3 = 0 ✓ |
| [SU(2)]²U(1)_Y | 0 ✓ (unchanged) |
| [U(1)_Y]³ | 1/36 − 1/4 − 8/9 + 1/9 + 1 = 0 ✓ |
| Gravitational | 0 ✓ (unchanged) |

All four conditions are satisfied. The right-handed content is uniquely determined — no other choice of representations restores all four conditions simultaneously.

**Theorem (Anomaly Determination):** The right-handed fermion content u_R, d_R, e_R is uniquely fixed by requiring anomaly cancellation given the T1-sector left-handed content Q_L and L. These are precisely the representations produced by the T2-sector chirality mechanism of Part I [citation].

The paper thus has a closed logical structure: the geometry produces the left-handed content, consistency requires the right-handed content, and the dynamics (Part I) produces it.

## 6. Three Generations from Triality

### 6.1 The Albert Algebra

The Albert algebra J₃(𝕆) is the unique exceptional Jordan algebra — the algebra of 3×3 Hermitian matrices with octonionic entries:

$$M = \begin{pmatrix} \alpha & x & \bar{y} \\ \bar{x} & \beta & z \\ y & \bar{z} & \gamma \end{pmatrix}, \qquad \alpha,\beta,\gamma \in \mathbb{R},\quad x,y,z \in \mathbb{O}$$

This is a 27-dimensional real space. Its automorphism group is F₄ and its structure group (conformal symmetries) is E₆.

### 6.2 Matter Content in J₃(𝕆)

Each off-diagonal entry z ∈ 𝕆 decomposes under SU(3) as:

$$z = z^7 e_7 + z^a q_a + \bar{z}^a \bar{q}_a$$

The triplet part z^a q_a carries colour — it is the quark sector. The singlet part z⁷e₇ carries no colour — it is the lepton sector. The T1-sector spinor provides the SU(2) doublet structure on each.

The three off-diagonal entries x, y, z each contribute one generation:

| Entry | Quark sector | Lepton sector |
|---|---|---|
| x | x^a q_a → Q_L^{(1)} | x⁷e₇ → L^{(1)} |
| y | y^a q_a → Q_L^{(2)} | y⁷e₇ → L^{(2)} |
| z | z^a q_a → Q_L^{(3)} | z⁷e₇ → L^{(3)} |

### 6.3 Triality and Three Generations

The Dynkin diagram of SO(8) has a Z₃ symmetry — **triality** — cycling its three 8-dimensional representations:

$$\tau : \mathbf{8}_v \rightarrow \mathbf{8}_s \rightarrow \mathbf{8}_c \rightarrow \mathbf{8}_v$$

On J₃(𝕆), the triality automorphism acts as a cyclic permutation of the off-diagonal entries:

$$\tau : x \rightarrow y \rightarrow z \rightarrow x$$

This permutation preserves the Freudenthal determinant:

$$\text{det}(M) = \alpha\beta\gamma - \alpha|z|^2 - \beta|y|^2 - \gamma|x|^2 + 2\,\text{Re}(xyz)$$

since each term maps to the next under simultaneous cycling of (α,β,γ) and (x,y,z). The three generations are therefore a single **orbit under triality** — they are one generation and its two triality images.

### 6.4 Why Exactly Three

The Z₃ group has order 3, giving an orbit of exactly 3 elements. A fourth generation would require J₄(𝕆) — a 4×4 octonionic Hermitian matrix algebra. By the Jordan-von Neumann-Wigner theorem (1934), formally real Jordan algebras over 𝕆 exist only for matrix size n ≤ 3. The case n = 3 is maximal and exceptional. A fourth generation is **algebraically excluded**.

**Lemma (Three Generations):** The matter content of the T1-sector, embedded in the off-diagonal entries of J₃(𝕆), admits exactly three generations under the triality automorphism of SO(8). The bound is sharp: formally real octonionic Jordan algebras exist only for n ≤ 3, with n = 3 the unique maximal case.

### 6.5 Generation Mixing

The cubic Freudenthal term 2Re(xyz) mixes all three generations. Under SU(3) decomposition, the purely triplet contribution involves the octonionic structure constants ε_{abc}:

$$\text{Re}(x^a q_a)(y^b q_b)(z^c q_c) \propto \varepsilon_{abc}\, x^a y^b z^c$$

This coupling is antisymmetric in generation indices and SU(3)-invariant. It is the geometric origin of inter-generation mixing. The CKM and PMNS matrices encode this coupling, constrained by the octonionic ε_{abc}. The explicit constraint on mixing angles is left for future work.

### 6.6 Mass Hierarchy

Triality is a symmetry of the kinematic structure — the Jordan algebra — but not of the dynamics. Mass differences between generations arise from the T2 coupling (Part I), which is not required to respect triality. The mass hierarchy is a dynamical question beyond the present framework.

## 7. The Embedding Spin(2,3) × G₂ ⊂ E₆

### 7.1 The Single Choice Theorem

Before establishing the embedding, we state the central theorem of Part II.

**Theorem (Single Choice):** The construction of SM matter content from Spin(2,3) × G₂ requires exactly one input: the choice of U(1)_Y inside Spin(2,3) × G₂. This choice simultaneously determines:

- The preferred timelike direction t₂ in ℝ^{2,3}
- The preferred imaginary octonion e₇ in ℝ⁷
- The T1/T2 split of the spinor
- The SU(3) colour group as the stabiliser of e₇
- The hypercharge values of all SM representations

Furthermore, U(1)_Y is the unique U(1) inside Spin(2,3) × G₂ satisfying anomaly cancellation with nonzero projection on both factors. The choice is canonical up to overall sign convention.

*Proof sketch:* The projection on the Spin(2,3) factor gives J^{01} with coefficient α. The projection on the G₂ factor gives Q₇ with coefficient β. Section 4.6 shows α = 1 and β = 2 are uniquely fixed by the SM hypercharge conditions on Q_L and L. Anomaly cancellation then follows from Section 5. Any U(1) with zero projection on either factor fails to produce both quark and lepton representations simultaneously. □

### 7.2 The Primitive Idempotent

A primitive idempotent of J₃(𝕆) is a rank-1 projector P satisfying P² = P, tr(P) = 1. These are the points of the octonionic projective plane 𝕆P².

Under the spacetime-octonion correspondence of Section 4.3, the time arrow t₁ = e₀ corresponds to the **canonical primitive idempotent**:

$$P_0 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \in J_3(\mathbb{O})$$

This is the Jordan algebra element corresponding to the real unit — the algebraically distinguished direction fixed by all of G₂ and corresponding to the time arrow in spacetime.

### 7.3 The Stabiliser Chain

By the Chevalley-Schafer theorem, the structure group of J₃(𝕆) is E₆. The stabiliser of a primitive idempotent P₀ inside E₆ is Spin(9) in the compact case or Spin(8,1) in the relevant split case, of dimension 36.

Inside Spin(8,1), the triality decomposition of SO(8) gives three 8-dimensional representations **8_v**, **8_s**, **8_c**. Under the embedding Spin(2,3) × G₂ ⊂ Spin(8):

$$\mathbf{8}_v = \mathbf{5}_{SO(2,3)} \oplus \mathbf{3}_{SU(3)}$$

$$\mathbf{8}_s = \mathbf{4}_{Spin(2,3)} \oplus \bar{\mathbf{4}}_{Spin(2,3)}$$

$$\mathbf{8}_c = \mathbf{4}'_{Spin(2,3)} \oplus \bar{\mathbf{4}}'_{Spin(2,3)}$$

The 4-component spinor of Spin(2,3) from Section 2 is precisely the **8_s** representation of Spin(8) restricted to Spin(2,3) × G₂. The T1/T2 split of the spinor corresponds to the **8_s/8_c** triality pair.

The dimension check confirms the embedding:

$$\dim(Spin(2,3) \times G_2) = 10 + 14 = 24 < 36 = \dim(Spin(8,1))$$

### 7.4 The Full Embedding Chain

$$SU(3)_c \times SU(2)_K \times U(1)_Y \subset Spin(2,3) \times G_2 \subset Spin(8) \subset Spin(8,1) \subset E_6$$

with dimensions:

$$12 \subset 24 \subset 28 \subset 36 \subset 78$$

| Inclusion | Geometric meaning |
|---|---|
| SM ⊂ Spin(2,3)×G₂ | Single choice theorem |
| Spin(2,3)×G₂ ⊂ Spin(8) | Triality decomposition |
| Spin(8) ⊂ Spin(8,1) | Stabiliser of primitive idempotent |
| Spin(8,1) ⊂ E₆ | Structure group of Albert algebra |

**Theorem (Embedding):** The group Spin(2,3) × G₂ embeds in E₆ as a subgroup of the stabiliser of a primitive idempotent of J₃(𝕆), via the triality decomposition of Spin(8). The SM gauge group SU(3)_c × SU(2)_K × U(1)_Y embeds canonically in E₆ via this chain. The embedding is determined by the single choice of U(1)_Y.

## 8. Summary and Discussion

### 8.1 What Has Been Derived

Starting from Spin(2,3) and the octonions, with a single canonical choice of U(1)_Y, we have derived:

1. **The SM gauge group** SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) as a subgroup of E₆ via the chain of Section 7.4
2. **The left-handed matter representations** Q_L = (3,2)_{+1/6} and L = (1,2)_{-1/2} from the T1-sector spinor tensored with the octonion internal space
3. **The hypercharge formula** Y = J^{01} + 2Q₇ with coefficients uniquely fixed
4. **The right-handed fermion content** u_R, d_R, e_R uniquely determined by anomaly cancellation
5. **Exactly three generations** from the triality automorphism of J₃(𝕆), with a fourth generation algebraically excluded
6. **Inter-generation mixing** from the cubic Freudenthal term Re(xyz)
7. **The ambient exceptional geometry** E₆ as the natural home of the construction

### 8.2 What Has Been Assumed

Two inputs are taken from Part I [citation] without derivation here:

- **The T1-sector projection:** Physical observable states correspond to the T1-sector of the Spin(2,3) spinor. The T2-sector governs the chirality and mass coupling mechanism.
- **The T2-sector matter content:** The right-handed fermions u_R, d_R, e_R are produced by the T2 chirality mechanism, consistent with the anomaly determination of Section 5.

One canonical choice is made within Part II:

- **The choice of U(1)_Y** inside Spin(2,3) × G₂, shown to be unique by the single choice theorem.

### 8.3 Deferred Questions

Several questions are identified but not resolved here:

- **The CKM and PMNS matrices:** The octonionic structure constants ε_{abc} constrain inter-generation mixing angles. The explicit constraint is left for future work.
- **The mass hierarchy:** Why the third generation is heavier than the first is a dynamical question requiring the full Part I framework.
- **Neutrino masses:** The geometric picture accommodates the neutrino as the SU(2) partner of the electron but does not explain the smallness of its mass without additional input.
- **The cosmological implications of the T2 sector:** In particular the dark matter candidate identified in Part I as the T2-sector fermion.

### 8.4 Relation to Existing Work

The use of octonions in connection with the Standard Model has a long history. Günaydin and Gürsey (1973) first identified SU(3) as the stabiliser of an octonion direction. Dixon (1994) developed the algebra ℝ⊗ℂ⊗ℍ⊗𝕆 as a unified framework. Baez (2002) gave a comprehensive account of the octonions and their role in exceptional Lie groups. Todorov and Dubois-Violette (2018) showed that the SM gauge group arises naturally from the octonion algebra.

The present work differs from these in three respects. First, the spacetime group Spin(2,3) plays a central role — the SM gauge structure arises from the interplay of spacetime and internal symmetry, not from the octonions alone. Second, the T1/T2 split of the two time dimensions provides a geometric origin for chirality, connecting to the companion framework of Part I. Third, the single choice theorem gives a precise statement of what is canonical and what is chosen in the construction.

## Appendix C: The Freudenthal Determinant Under Triality

The Freudenthal determinant det(M) = αβγ − α|z|² − β|y|² − γ|x|² + 2Re(xyz) is invariant under the triality permutation τ: (α,x) → (β,y) → (γ,z) → (α,x). Under τ:

- αβγ → βγα = αβγ ✓
- α|z|² → β|x|² and β|y|² → γ|z|² and γ|x|² → α|y|², so the sum is preserved ✓
- Re(xyz) → Re(yzx) = Re(xyz) by cyclicity of Re on octonion products ✓

The determinant is therefore a triality invariant, confirming the three generations have identical kinematic structure. □
