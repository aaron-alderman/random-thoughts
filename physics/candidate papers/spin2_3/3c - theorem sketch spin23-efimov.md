# Theorem Sketch: Faddeev Collective Mode as SO(2,1) Casimir Eigenstate in Spin(2,3)

**Statement:** The symmetric collective eigenvalue \(\lambda_{\rm sym}\) isolated in the three-boson Faddeev decomposition at unitarity equals (up to normalization) the Casimir eigenvalue of the SO(2,1) subgroup acting on the three-state collective transport mode formed from near-boundary Spin(2,3) sectors.

**Setup (from master framework & compendium):**
- Spin(2,3) ≅ Sp(4,ℝ) spinors split as T1 ⊕ T2 under \(J^{01}\).
- Near persistence/locking boundaries, three simultaneous transport states (constructive/inverted/frustrated combinations) form a collective radial mode whose dynamics linearize to \(\dot{R} \approx \varepsilon R\).
- This linearization is generated by the dilatation operator of the embedded SO(2,1) ≅ SL(2,ℝ).

**Faddeev side (from 1a–1c, 3–3b):**
- Wave function decomposed into pair channels \(\phi_{ij}\).
- Symmetric sector yields 3×3 kinematic recoupling matrix.
- Symmetric eigenvector gives \(\lambda_{\rm sym} = \varepsilon_0 + 2W_0 = -s_0^2 - 1/4\), where \(s_0\) solves the transcendental equation from the Mellin-transformed cross-channel kernel.
- Hyperradial equation after Langer reduction \(F = f/\rho^{5/2}\) becomes effective Schrödinger with \(U_{\rm eff} = -(s_0^2 + 1/4)/\rho^2\).

**Bridge:**
The three-pair channel matrix is the explicit matrix representation of the tensor product of three transport-class projectors restricted to the symmetric subspace. The kernel recoupling (angular integration in hyperspherical coordinates) is induced by the Spin(2,3) group action on the reduced phase space.

The SO(2,1) Casimir in the hyperradial coordinate acts precisely as the second-order differential operator whose eigenvalue on the collective mode is \(\lambda(\lambda-1)\) with \(\lambda = is_0 + 1/2\) (or equivalent real form after Wick rotation to the bound-state regime). Thus:
\[
\lambda_{\rm sym} = C_{\rm SO(2,1)} - \tfrac{1}{4},
\]
where the \(-1/4\) is the universal Langer shift in radial reduction.

**Proof outline (sketch):**
1. Embed the Faddeev channel basis into the three-fold tensor product of the 2-component T1/T2 sectors.
2. The Bethe–Peierls boundary condition (zero-range) corresponds to the projection onto the massless T1-like boundary traversal.
3. The symmetric eigenmode is the Spin(2,3)-invariant singlet under the residual symmetry at threshold.
4. The recoupling kernel is the matrix element of the SO(2,1) generator in this basis → its eigenvalues reproduce the known transcendental equation for \(s_0\).

**Corollaries:**
- Discrete scaling ratio is fixed by the group Casimir (independent of short-distance details → universality).
- Turning on T1 ↔ T2 mixing adds a finite-range perturbation that shifts the three-body parameter without destroying the tower.
